Tømreren på Orø

Futurenow is a financial service concept designed as part of my master thesis project in collaboration with Danske Bank Group’s Customer Engagement department.

The challenge was to design a financial service which could potentially ease negative feelings associated with unexpected expenses for young adults, as this target group is often impacted by expenses they either has forgotten or has not accounted for due to a relative low liquidity.

The project was developed with two other fantastic individuals from my univeristy. Scroll down to see a brief overview of the design process.

1

Understanding client and context

In order to gain a better understanding of who we are designing for, we took a field trip to the local island where the carpenter operates and was shown several of his work, whilest he explains about his field of work.

2

Idea generating on data

Looking at the field trip and interview data, sketches was drawn to iterate on the different ways the website could represent the key functionality needed for the carpenter. Subsequently, these sketches were digitally wireframed.

3

Usability test

These wireframes were then utilised as the foundation for conducting a usability test (Think aloud test) to determine how well potential users would navigate the website.

Final concept

The final concept is a service concept, which is placed within the Mobile Bank. With the flick of switch the user can switch to a future view which allows them to see their bank accounts balance a week, month and a year ahead.

In addition, the user can see what attributes to said account balance, which allows the user to not only be reminded of unforseen expenses, but also gain an understanding of what constitutes their economy.

Lastly, the concept will also generate insights, which informs the user on various economic subject relevant to their situation.

By allowing users to gain insights onto their economic situation and the different components consituting it, the service concept should hopefully ease the negative feelings associated with unexpeced expenses through foresight.
