Carpenter on Orø.

Website design.   User experience.   Static web development.

The Carpenter on Orø, was a project which revolved around designing a website for a local carpenter. The project was developed as part of a university course ’’Designing Digital Platforms’’.

The challenge was simply to design a website for person or company, while demonstrating basic UX, UI and web development competences. This means the needs of potential customers for the carpenter and the carpenter himself was identified and translated to website functionality through evocative sketching and usability testing.

The project was developed with three other individuals enrolled in the course. Scroll down to see a brief overview of the design process.

1

Understanding client and context

In order to gain a better understanding of who we are designing for, we took a field trip to the local island where the carpenter operates and was shown several of his work, whilst he explains about his field of work.

2

Idea generating on data

Looking at the field trip and interview data, sketches was drawn to iterate on the different ways the website could represent the key functionality needed for the carpenter. Subsequently, these sketches were digitally wireframed.

3

Usability test

These wireframes were then utilised as the foundation for conducting a usability test (Think aloud test) to determine how well potential users would navigate the website.

Final concept

The research showed that the carpenter believed it was important he could show of his work on his website, since he was highly specialized in certain type of carpentry. In conjunction with the potential users expressing the primary need being able to quickly contact the carpenter and that the carpenter had a high level of trust, the website was designed around these principles.
