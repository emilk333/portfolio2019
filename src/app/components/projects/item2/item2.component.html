VIP redesign.

Service design.    Design thinking.    UI design.

The VIP redesign was a design challenge issued by innovations consultants associated with the Rigshospital’s pediactric care unit, and was related to a course at the IT-university revolving around Service design.

The challenge was to identify a solution, which enabled an increased use of an internal digital document system named VIP. The VIP system is utilized by nurses and other medical staff at danish hospitals to look up procedures and protocols for practice, which meant a lack of usage could potentially lead to a reduction of patient care quality.

By identifying the causes for the lack of usage, we were able to propose a designed solution which would increase the VIP usage and ultimately creating a better experience for both the nurses everyday life as well as the patients.

The project was developed with 5 other individuals inrolled in the course. Scroll down to see a brief overview of the design process.

1

Understanding the problem area

After gaining an understanding the initial design challenge, we conducted interviews with the administrative staff of the hospital as well as field observations, which involved following nurses in their daily work. This resulted in identifying three problem areas: Accesibility, Readability and Credibility.

2

User data analysis

The insights gained from the field observations and interviews laid the foundations for developing eco-system maps, scenarios and personas, service blueprint, and user journeys. These were used to draw a picture of who we are designing for.

3

Generating ideas

Having gained an understanding for who we are designing for and the surrounding context, a barrier analysis combined with ’’How might we’’ questions were utilised. Subsequently ideas were sketched and selected via a 10 plus 10 idea generation methodology.

Final concept

The final concept resulted in the following 6 design suggestions:

1.   Suggestions within the Healthcare Platform
2.   Suggestions within the VIP system
3.   Consistency in VIP documents
4.   A short and elaborated version of a VIP document
5.   Feedback mechanism within the VIP system
6.   Awareness regarding updated VIP documents

Adapting and implementing these suggestions into the VIP system, the challenges of Accesibility, Readability and Credibility should potentially be tackled. This would ultimately enable better patient care through an improved VIP system usage in the everyday practice of the nurses.
